v0.8 Editorial project list — final direction Jun 2026
~ ChangedLucía

Rejected all card-based layouts. Requested an editorial, clean, intuitive direction — no background images in the project grid.

"I don't like this design at all. Can you make a new proposal, different from everything before, without background images? I'm looking for an editorial, clean, intuitive style."
+ AddedClaude

Replaced the card grid with a magazine index list: hairline dividers, numbered rows in italic serif, project title at editorial scale, tags and a CTA button flush right. Accent colour (#51FFA1) reserved for hover state only — neon sweep fills the row background on interaction. Cover images removed from the layout and repurposed as cursor-following previews on hover.

~ ChangedLucía

Cursor preview removed. Design log updated to 8 versions. Requested full deployable folder.

"Remove the hover image. Update the design log. Give me the whole folder with everything I need."
− RemovedClaude

Cursor-following image preview removed from #s-work. The list now communicates project identity through typography and tags alone — consistent with the editorial restraint of the direction.

v0.7 Cover images — background vs. hover preview Jun 2026
+ AddedLucía

Supplied new cover images as JPGs with correct brand background colours (pink, mint green, cream) to replace earlier dark-background PNGs.

"Use the new images I'm giving you — and respect the transparencies for white backgrounds."
~ ChangedClaude

Attempted three strategies: (1) 2-col white card grid with cover images as object-fit: cover; (2) restored original index with images injected as per-card background-image via CSS; (3) editorial list with images attached only to cursor hover. All three rejected or iterated — led directly to v0.8.

v0.6 Changelog drawer — professional rewrite Jun 2026
~ ChangedLucía

Prompt log rewritten as versioned changelog with stronger visual structure and professional tone.

"The prompt log drawer needs to be more professional in the writing. I need it to be more visual."
~ ChangedClaude

Redesigned drawer: collapsible version blocks, diff stripe system (green add / yellow change / red remove / grey note), monospace metadata header, prompt quotes separated visually from analysis. Writing register shifted to technical audit trail — consistent with the AI-collaboration angle of the portfolio.

v0.5 AI transparency layer Jun 2026
+ AddedLucía

Requested stronger AI collaboration signal to impress hiring managers and attract clients who want AI-forward designers.

"How can we make it more impactful in terms of AI collaboration?"
+ AddedClaude

Built prompt log drawer: persistent side tab with pulsing dot, exposing the real design conversation as a versioned changelog. Connected to guided chat — the "fellow designer" path opens the drawer directly, turning the transparency mechanism itself into a portfolio talking point.

v0.4 Project grid — 4 attempts before a direction landed Jun 2026
− RemovedLucía

Horizontal scroll, bento grid, and image-led 2-column rejected in sequence.

"I still don't like the grid for the projects. I would like them to be more visual, bigger. Forget about the images — just create something with the typography and colors. Something new."
+ AddedClaude

Designed typographic river: full-width colour blocks stacked vertically, no images. Project name rendered at editorial scale (up to 9rem) as the sole visual. Brand colours derived from each client's identity: #BF0000 Madrid, #095F34 CBRE, #748F48 Respirapp, #FFCA01 Yay!, #33CC33 Foodizon, #E10E49 Aliseda. Accepted — held until v0.7 cover image integration prompted a rethink.

v0.3 Architecture — full-screen snap sections Jun 2026

v0.1 Initial concept — AI conversational portfolio Jun 2026
+ AddedLucía

Brief: build a portfolio that showcases AI-assisted UX/UI design. The portfolio itself should be the demonstration — not a page about AI, but a page built with it.

"I would like this portfolio to be AI based. This will be a showcase of how to use AI to create a UX/UI project — the project itself will be this portfolio."
+ AddedClaude

Proposed concept: guided conversational hero (simulated chat with Lucía as the AI persona) + typefaces Noticia Text + Noto Sans. Palette: #454545 text, #51FFA1 accent, #F3F3F3 surface. The accent green connects the AI badge, nav active states, and interactive elements into a coherent system without feeling branded.

About

Strategic thinking.
Human focus.

I'm a UX/UI designer who works at the intersection of research, strategy, and craft. My process is rooted in Design Thinking — understanding systems and people deeply before proposing solutions — and increasingly shaped by AI as a design collaborator, not just a tool.

I've worked across sectors where the stakes are real: public services used by millions, enterprise tools that drive revenue, and digital products that touch health and wellbeing. I bring the same rigour to every scale of problem.

For 4 years I was lead UX/UI teacher at Ironhack Bootcamp, across Madrid, Berlin, and the full remote Europe campus — where I learned that the best way to understand design is to teach it.
